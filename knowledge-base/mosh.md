Hi Shubham upadhyay, 👋



You've probably been hearing a lot about Claude Code lately. It's everywhere — X, YouTube, developer communities. And if you're not sure what it is, here's the short version: it's an AI coding agent that lives in your terminal. You describe what you want to build, and it plans, writes, and iterates on the code autonomously. It's not autocomplete. It's closer to having a developer working alongside you around the clock.



Anyway — when I first started using it, I had this moment where I just sat back and watched it build an entire feature from scratch. Like, I described what I wanted, and it just... did it. Wrote the code, ran it, fixed the errors, iterated. I barely touched the keyboard.



And my first reaction wasn't pure excitement. It was more complicated than that. Part of me was amazed. Another part of me was genuinely unsettled. I kept thinking — what does this mean for developers? What does this mean for the craft I've spent years practicing?



I sat with that feeling for a while. And then I kept using it. And I started to see that the developers who are going to thrive with these tools aren't the ones who hand everything over to the AI and hope for the best. They're the ones who stay in control. Who bring their engineering judgment to the table. Who use AI to move faster without sacrificing the things that make software good.



Once I figured that out, everything changed. I built a full-stack, production-ready app — authentication, database, AI features, email integration, deployed to production — in about 2 days. Something that would have taken me 2-3 weeks typing it all out manually. And I was proud of what I built. I understood every part of it. I owned it.



That experience is what this course is about.



It's called Claude Code for Professional Developers, and it's live today. I'll show you exactly how I did that — from a blank folder to a fully deployed app — the right way, without cutting corners.



You can check it out here: https://codewithmosh.com/p/claude-code
